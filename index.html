About

I am a French robotics researcher based in Tokyo, currently serving as Project Assistant Professor at the Embodied Media Project (KMD), Keio University, and Visiting Researcher at Waseda University (Iwata Lab) and Honda Research Institute Japan.

My research focuses on teleoperation, embodiment, and human-in-the-loop control — specifically on how humans can seamlessly transfer motor skills to robotic systems through intuitive interfaces, haptic feedback, and impedance adaptation. At Honda, I developed bimanual teleoperation systems for next-generation avatar platforms.

I hold a PhD from the University of Montpellier / LIRMM (2022), funded by the JST ERATO project. I am now focused on applied academic research in teleoperation and human motor control — contributing to the JST Moonshot program on VR, neurosciences and robotics at KMD.

I collaborate closely with the Inami Lab at UTokyo — where my work on supernumerary robotic limbs (Co-Limbs) originated.

I am fluent in French, English, and Japanese (日本語) — and I bring a cross-cultural perspective to both research and collaboration across European and Asian institutions.
