About

I am a French robotics researcher based in Tokyo, currently serving as Project Assistant Professor at the Embodied Media Project (KMD), Keio University, and Visiting Researcher at Waseda University (Iwata Lab) and Honda Research Institute Japan.

My research focuses on teleoperation, embodiment, and human-in-the-loop control — specifically on how humans can seamlessly transfer motor skills to robotic systems through intuitive interfaces, haptic feedback, and impedance adaptation. At Honda, I developed bimanual teleoperation systems for next-generation avatar platforms.

I hold a PhD from the University of Montpellier / LIRMM (2022), funded by the JST ERATO project. I am now focused on applied academic research in teleoperation and human motor control — contributing to the JST Moonshot program on VR, neurosciences and robotics at KMD.

I collaborate closely with the Inami Lab at UTokyo — where my work on supernumerary robotic limbs (Co-Limbs) originated.

I am fluent in French, English, and Japanese (日本語) — and I bring a cross-cultural perspective to both research and collaboration across European and Asian institutions.

ACM SIGGRAPH 2024
Miruoto — Seeing Sound at the Stadium
Real-time AI visual rendering of sports atmosphere · Waseda University / JST · 2022–2024
Live at the Deaflympics Finals Real-time system · Nov 2025
Project Overview ACM SIGGRAPH 2024

Miruoto (ミルオト — "seeing sound") is a real-time AI system that makes the atmosphere of live sports events visible for Deaf and hard-of-hearing spectators — translating crowd noise and player motion into an affective visual rendering. Presented as an Emerging Technologies demo at ACM SIGGRAPH 2024 in Denver, the system was subsequently deployed live during the championship finals of the Tokyo Deaflympics 2025 (November 2025), running in real time on venue screens alongside the match.

Publication G. Gourmelen, S. Toriya, E. Miya, N. Shioura, H. Iwata. "Miruoto: Sports Event Atmosphere Visual Rendering through Real-time Image and Sound Processing." ACM SIGGRAPH 2024 — Emerging Technologies. doi:10.1145/3641517.3664393 ↗ · PDF ↗ · miruoto.jp ↗

IEEE IROS 2021
Impedance Adaptation — Tele-Operated Physical Assistance
Human guided trajectory & impedance estimation during HRI · LIRMM / Université de Montpellier · 2021
Project Video IEEE IROS 2021 · Replace IROS_VIDEO_URL

This work addresses a core challenge in teleoperation: transferring not just position but physical interaction quality from a human to a robotic system. The system estimates the human operator's arm impedance (stiffness and damping) online during task execution, then adapts the robot's impedance to match — so the robot replicates not only the trajectory but the compliance strategy the human chose for a given task. A 7-DoF robot arm was controlled via a 3-DoF haptic device, with real-time impedance adaptation demonstrated on physical assistance tasks.

Publication G. Gourmelen, B. Navarro, A. Cherubini, G. Ganesh. "Human Guided Trajectory and Impedance Adaptation for Tele-Operated Physical Assistance." IEEE IROS 2021. doi:10.1109/IROS51168.2021.9636418 ↗ · PDF ↗

ACM SIGGRAPH Asia 2019
Co-Limbs — Wearable Collaborative Robotic Arms
Supernumerary robotic limbs with intuitive shared control · UTokyo / LIRMM JST ERATO · 2019
Project Video ACM SIGGRAPH Asia 2019 · Replace COLIMBS_VIDEO_URL

Co-Limbs is a wearable supernumerary robotic arm designed to be worn and controlled intuitively — without dedicated hand controllers or explicit command inputs. The system decodes the wearer's intent from shoulder and wrist kinematics, activating a library of shared-control motion primitives (reach, grasp, hold) that blend human guidance with robotic force and precision. Developed during a research visit to the Inami Lab at the University of Tokyo, demonstrated live at ACM SIGGRAPH Asia 2019 Emerging Technologies (Brisbane). This work formed a core chapter of the doctoral thesis.

Publication G. Gourmelen, A. Verhulst, B. Navarro, T. Sasaki, G. Ganesh, M. Inami. "Co-Limbs: An Intuitive Collaborative Control for Wearable Robotic Arms." ACM SIGGRAPH Asia 2019 — Emerging Technologies. doi:10.1145/3355049.3360526 ↗ · PDF ↗
